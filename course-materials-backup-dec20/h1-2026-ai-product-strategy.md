# H1 2026 AI Product Strategy: TaskFlow

## DIAGNOSIS: The Resource-Constrained Arms Race

**The Situation:**
TaskFlow is positioned in a rapidly evolving market where primary competitors—Notion, Linear, and Asana—are aggressively integrating Gen AI. Notion focuses on knowledge management and agentic workflows, Linear on engineering velocity, and Asana on enterprise coordination. All are moving upmarket or targeting broad knowledge worker segments.

TaskFlow's core advantage is its simplicity for small teams (5-20 people) and its existing voice-centric infrastructure. However, we face extreme resource constraints: a team of only 2 engineers (non-ML specialists) and a $50k budget for H1 2026. While 15% of our users have tried initial AI features, adoption is moderate, and our company priority remains user activation and retention.

**The Crux:**
We must match the perceived feature velocity of better-funded competitors to remain relevant, while doubling down on our unique voice-first interaction model to win the underserved SMB segment that values speed and simplicity over enterprise complexity.

---

## GUIDING POLICY: Differentiated Velocity through Specialization

**Our Overall Approach:**
We will execute a "specialized velocity" strategy. Instead of building broad, mediocre AI features, we will focus our limited engineering resources on high-impact voice-to-task capabilities that leverage our existing infrastructure. We will match competitive pressure not by copying their entire roadmap, but by delivering a superior "AI-first" experience for our specific niche—teams that need to capture information on the move.

**Key Strategic Choices:**
- **Voice-First Focus:** We will prioritize voice as the primary interaction mode for AI, creating a Siri-like experience for task management.
- **Velocity Parity:** We will ship frequently to maintain the perception of speed, using Antigravity to multiply the output of our 2-engineer team.
- **Subsidized Adoption:** AI features will be included free in core plans to maximize the company's activation and retention goals.
- **Defensible Quality:** We will prioritize shipping a few high-quality, reliable features over many experimental ones.
- **We will NOT:** Build broad AI writing assistants or enterprise-grade reporting bots that compete directly with Notion or Asana.

---

## COHERENT ACTIONS: H1 2026 Roadmap

**Q1 2026: The Voice Foundation**
- **AI Voice-to-Task Engine:** Deploy "Siri for TaskFlow"—allowing users to create complex tasks, set due dates, and assign owners entirely via voice.
- **Whisper API Integration:** Utilize OpenAI's Whisper (leveraging our $50k budget) to ensure best-in-class transcription without needing in-house ML specialists.
- **Smart Activation Nudges:** Use AI to identify users struggling with project setup and offer automated, voice-guided onboarding.

**Q2 2026: Mobile & Retention**
- **AI Smart Daily Digest:** Generate personalized, voice-readable daily summaries of team activity to drive morning retention.
- **TaskFlow AI Marketplace:** Enable 3rd-party integrations (e.g., Slack/Notion) to import tasks via voice shortcuts, creating defensive moats through ecosystem connectivity.
- **Performance Optimization:** Spend 20% of engineering bandwidth on reducing AI latency to ensure the voice experience feels "live" and immediate.

**Resource Allocation:**
- **Engineering:** 100% of the 2-engineer team dedicated to the AI Voice-to-Task engine and Whisper integration.
- **Budget:** $30k allocated to API costs (OpenAI/Whisper/GPT-4o), $20k for infrastructure and specialized contract design support for voice UI.

**Critical Assumptions:**
- **Assumption 1:** SMB users value voice-driven speed enough to overlook the lack of broad document-editing AI.
- **Assumption 2:** A 2-engineer team can maintain high velocity using agentic coding tools like Antigravity.
- **Assumption 3:** API costs remain sustainable within the $50k envelope for the first 50k active users.
